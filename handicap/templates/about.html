About Handicap Indexes

Maintain a golf handicap index consistent with the World Handicap System (WHS).

Your current handicap index is calculated depending on how many rounds have been played. The number of rounds used to calculate the index will change as more rounds are played. With fewer than 20 rounds, less of your rounds will be used; once you have played 20 rounds, the best 8 of your 20 most recent rounds are used. A handicap is only calculated when at least three rounds are entered.

You may view the rounds used to calculate the handicap index under Handicap Index. You may view all the rounds you have entered under All Rounds. Use Add Round to add a new round.

For more information on the World Handicap System, please visit the R&A, the USGA, or the WHS handicapping authority in your country.
